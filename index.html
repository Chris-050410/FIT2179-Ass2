Takeaway Insight: Malaysia’s independent smallholders remain concentrated in traditional production strongholds, while East Malaysia’s role continues to expand in the national palm oil industry.

Different States, Different Growth Journeys

Key Insight: Malaysia’s palm oil industry was historically centred in traditional plantation regions, but future expansion is shifting toward East Malaysia’s growth potential. With larger land reserves, Sabah and Sarawak are gradually reshaping Malaysia’s future production balance.

Malaysia’s Palm Oil Future Is Moving East

Bars show each state’s national immature share
Key Insight: Although representing future plantation expansion, Malaysia’s immature plantation pattern only changed slightly between 2023 and 2025. This reflects how oil palm growth depends more on long-term investment and land availability, since new plantations require several years before becoming fully productive.

Malaysia Palm Oil Export Structure

One Product Powered Malaysia’s Palm Oil Export Growth

Insight Takeaway: Malaysia’s palm oil export growth was driven by higher-value activities within the supply chain, not simply producing more palm oil. As processed palm oil exports expanded, Malaysia became more connected to global refining and manufacturing networks instead of relying mainly on raw commodity trade.

Not All Palm Oil Products Shared the Same Export Story

Key Insight: Malaysia’s palm oil exports did not grow evenly across products. While some products achieved stable long-term growth, others remained volatile or expanded only later, reflecting changing global demand and downstream priorities. Note: Different y-axis ranges are used to better reveal the distinct growth patterns of products operating at very different export scales.

Malaysia in the Global Palm Oil Market

Indonesia Surged Ahead as Malaysia Stayed Steady

Takeaway Insight: Malaysia’s stable production trend reflects a stronger focus on sustainability and productivity efficiency rather than large-scale expansion. Unlike Indonesia’s scale-driven growth, Malaysia’s competitiveness increasingly depends on higher-value production.

Emerging Producers Outpaced Malaysia’s Growth

Key Insight: Malaysia’s slower production growth suggests a more mature industry, while emerging producers expanded faster through land expansion and easier scaling. This increases the importance of efficiency and higher-value production for Malaysia’s future competitiveness.

Processed Palm Oil Stayed Malaysia’s Export Backbone

Key Insight: Malaysia’s strength in processed palm oil exports reflects strong downstream processing capabilities and higher-value activities beyond raw palm oil production. During COVID-19 period, biodiesel exports declined while other downstream palm products continued gaining momentum, highlighting shifting global demand patterns.
